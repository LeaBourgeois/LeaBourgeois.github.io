We are 4 students of EMLyon Business School, and we made some research about the "Dark side of the hospital" the lacks of materials,staff, and difficulty of shcedule etc... and we made some interviews. Here is a sume up of our research and interiews. 

In 2017, the "Special Envoy" program revealed more than 26,000 incident records that should have remained confidential. Many of these incidents are small daily equipment failures, but some also report serious incidents. "Of all these documents, there are more than 1,000 that report problems with working conditions and lack of staff," reports the editor of "Mediacités".  The purpose of these sheets is to report a malfunction. But through them, it is the exasperation that shows through. For example, one of these cards tells of an episode where a gynecological nurse finds herself alone for a whole day with a caregiver caring for 14 patients. She writes that "the team is physically and morally exhausted" and talks about "feeling of work done badly and putting patients' lives at risk". This sheet shows the lack of staff but also the consequences of these precarious conditions on the hospital staff. 

According to another source, a hospital executive named Paul, the teams still lack resources. They must constantly justify why they need this or that accessory. It is because of the search for efficiency. It is necessary to avoid lapses and waste of equipment, which results in a just-in-time work between teammates. There is also a permanent pooling of material resources to negotiate and lower prices in order to obtain equipment at lower cost, but they are therefore of poorer quality, which does not help to optimize care.

At the time, they claimed that this method further reduced organizational resources and put employees in a dangerous situation. "We went so far as to predict a risk of suicide attempts," recalls one of the survey's authors. Unfortunately, the firm was right: six months after the report, four careers linked to the Toulouse University Hospital committed suicide in just four weeks.
